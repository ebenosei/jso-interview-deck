Proposal 1

Employee Engagement & Retention Analytics

JSO conducts biennial community surveys — but there's no publicly evident internal engagement measurement. With $100M+ invested in compensation, the agency needs data on whether it's working.

What I Would Build

01 Design a validated engagement survey using the Job Descriptive Index & Organizational Climate Questionnaire — instruments already used in JSO's prior OJP-funded assessment
02 Segment analysis by division, rank, and tenure — with special focus on corrections (highest vacancy rates and burnout indicators)
03 Build a predictive attrition model to identify at-risk employees before they separate — enabling targeted retention interventions
04 Deliver quarterly outcomes reports to leadership with actionable recommendations and ROI tracking against the FOP contract investment
04 / 11

Proposal 2

Selection Validation & Adverse Impact Analysis

JSO's selection process spans 8+ stages. With Hispanic representation at 4.8% vs. 8.3% countywide, and NAACP scrutiny of leadership diversity, ongoing validation is essential for legal defensibility.

What I Would Build

01 Conduct 4/5ths rule adverse impact analysis at each selection stage — written test, physical ability, oral board, polygraph, and psych evaluation
02 Identify where diverse candidates are disproportionately screened out and propose evidence-based remediation strategies
03 Validate selection instruments against on-the-job performance criteria: academy completion, FTO ratings, 1/3/5-year retention
04 Produce technical validation reports with criterion-related validity evidence to support legal compliance and EEO standards
05 / 11

Proposal 3

POWER Program Evaluation Partnership

JSO's POWER wellness initiative (Peace Officer Wellness, Empathy & Resilience) has UCLA-backed research showing improvements in mindfulness, emotion regulation, and decreased stress. As it scales to 450+ officers, JSO needs an in-house research partner.

What I Would Build

01 Design pre/post psychometric measurement protocols using validated instruments — tracking mindfulness, burnout, stress, and resilience scales
02 Track longitudinal organizational outcomes: sick leave usage, use-of-force incidents, citizen complaints, retention rates — POWER participants vs. controls
03 Produce annual outcomes reports quantifying ROI to justify continued investment to leadership and external funders
04 Position JSO as a national model for evidence-based officer wellness — publishable findings for the Journal of Community Safety and Well-being
